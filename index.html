About

To me, travel has been a big part of my life. This map is a log of all the airports I have taken flights to and from.
Each orange dot represents an airport. Hovering over it shows you the name of the airport and municipality it is located in.
The lines depict the flight paths. They are 'geodesic', meaning that they show the true geographical shortest route.
A route more similar the one an airplane would take as opposed to just drawing a straight line on this map.
The geodesic lines add aesthetic appeal to the map but also give one an idea of just how distorted unprojected maps are.
The lines are colored blue and yellow for intranational and international flights. Being a washington state resident and
in close proximity to a large international airport in Vancouver, I take advantage of cheaper international ticket fares
and fly out of the Vancouver International Airport. For flights across the nation I use the 'Seatac' airport also known
as the Seattle-Tacoma Airport. When traveling to a European country from North America, often times you'll have a
layover in another country. Also, taking small flights known as 'puddle-jumpers' to close countries accounts for part
of my across europe travel.
